CHARACTERS IN NOLI ME TANGERE

Sisa

 
           

  • In the book, two little boys named Crispin and Basilio have Sisa as their mother.
    • 

  • She is portrayed as a woman who was once lovely and content but has been damaged 
by adversity.
    • 

  • Tandang Selo, Sisa's elderly husband, frequently leaves the house and leaves her 
in charge of raising their children.
    •                    

  • Her persona serves as a representation of the persecution and suffering that the 
Filipino people endured while living under Spanish control.
    •

Basillo

 
           

  • Basilio is Crispin's brother because he is the child of Sisa and Tandang Selo.
    • 

  • He is portrayed as a young boy who is intelligent and diligent and who has a tremendous 
desire to better his life and the lives of his family.
    • 

  • Basilio assists Father Salvi, the curate, in his duties as a sacristan at the church.
    • 

  • As a result of Basilio's portrayal of the struggles and aspirations of Filipino youth 
under Spanish colonial rule, he comes to embody the possibility of liberation and 
transformation.
    •

Crispin

 
           

  • Crispin is Basilio's younger sibling and the child of Sisa and Tandang Selo.
    • 

  • He is presented as a shy and submissive young kid, in contrast to his more diligent 
and aspirational sibling, Basilio.
    • 

  • Crispin assists his brother Basilio in his responsibilities as a sacristan at the church.
    • 

  • Crispin's unfortunate experience serves to highlight social problems and societal and religious 
corruption, enhancing the Filipino people's desire for justice and progress.
    •
